How to Use This Project

Start with the strongest pages, then widen out

  • Start with a flagship passage to see the project at its strongest and most balanced.
  • Read the overview first, then move into supporting claims where they exist.
  • Use the methodology page to understand the six evaluation criteria.
  • Treat non-flagship work as in progress rather than as finished representative analysis.

Methodology

Six criteria and multiple perspectives

The project compares Christian, skeptical, and Jewish readings while asking whether a claim is prior, predictive, non-mundane, specific, clear, and independent.

Open the methodology page for the full criteria and the new page status definitions.

Flagship Passages

Featured Flagship Analyses

These are the clearest starting points for beta readers. Each page is intended to show the project’s current standard for developed passage-level analysis.

Flagship Analysis

Genesis 3:15

Seed of the Woman. An early canonical and typological test case for how later messianic readings emerge from primordial conflict language.

Flagship Analysis

Isaiah 7:14

Immanuel. A defining birth-text dispute centered on Ahaz’s crisis, the meaning of almah, and Matthew’s reuse of the verse.

Flagship Analysis

Isaiah 53

Suffering Servant. The central servant-song debate over suffering, atonement, servant identity, and prophetic method.

Flagship Analysis

Psalm 22

Righteous Sufferer. A cumulative-reading case where lament poetry, Gospel reuse, and fulfillment method all meet.

Flagship Analysis

Micah 5:2

Bethlehem Ruler. A compact specificity test focused on Bethlehem, Davidic identity, and restoration hope.

Flagship Analysis

Daniel 9

Seventy Weeks. The project’s major chronological test case, where apocalyptic symbolism and date calculations do most of the work.

Flagship Analysis

Zechariah 11

Thirty Pieces of Silver. A vivid narrative-overlap passage that tests whether fulfillment is predicted or scripturally framed.
