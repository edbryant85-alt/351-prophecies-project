This line belongs to the poem's transition from humiliation to vindication. It reinforces the idea that the servant's suffering has purpose and effect beyond the suffering itself.

## Claimed New Testament Fulfillment
Christian readers relate this claim to passages such as John 12:27 and to broader New Testament themes in which Jesus' suffering fulfills God's purpose. The linkage is theological and retrospective rather than an explicit NT quotation of Isaiah 53:11a.

## Christian Interpretation
